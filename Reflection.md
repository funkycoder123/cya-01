# Reflection — CYA-01

**Student:** Lucky Chiemerie Nwachukwu
**Adventure:** Software Engineering Internship Applications — Summer 2025

## Activity Description

For this adventure, I chose to apply to software engineering internships for Summer 2025. The assignment asked me to select a career-aligned experience, produce a verifiable deliverable, and reflect critically on my decisions and professional growth. I committed to a structured job search: identifying target companies, refining my application materials, and submitting applications to backend and full-stack engineering roles. Over the course of this activity, I applied to over 40 positions across companies of varying sizes — from logistics-focused startups to large tech firms — using LinkedIn, Handshake, and direct company career portals and github links.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

The most significant decision I made was to lead with TruckTap as my primary project signal across every application. TruckTap is a logistics management web platform I built independently, featuring a Django/PostgreSQL backend, 40+ REST API endpoints, JWT authentication with role-based access control, WebSocket-based real-time chat, and a full CI/CD pipeline via GitHub Actions with 100+ automated tests. Rather than listing it generically as "a web app," I made the deliberate decision to quantify every detail — 40 endpoints, sub-1% authentication error rate in staging, 60-second reduction in quote time — because I knew screeners respond to specificity and measurable impact.

I also chose to apply strategically rather than broadly. I prioritized roles where my stack was a direct match (Django, PostgreSQL, REST APIs, Docker, AWS) and avoided frontend-heavy roles where my profile would be weaker. For logistics or API-first companies, I drew an explicit line from TruckTap to their domain in cover letters, which I believe significantly strengthened those applications.

## Contributions

I worked independently throughout this process. I sourced all listings, wrote and revised all application materials myself, and made every targeting decision without a template. I used WSU Career Services resources for resume structure feedback and conducted peer review on cover letter language before submitting final versions.

## Quality Assessment

I'm satisfied with the intentionality and targeting of my applications. Forcing myself to articulate why each company fit my profile sharpened how I think about my own skills and career direction. The area I would most improve is tracking discipline — I began applying before setting up a systematic log, which made it difficult to follow up on early submissions. In every future job search, I will set up a tracker before submitting a single application so that every deadline, contact name, and status update is captured from day one.

## Career Alignment

This experience is the most direct step I could take toward both of my stated career goals. A Summer 2025 internship would give me the production engineering experience, mentorship, and resume depth that companies like Apple require in new-grad backend engineers. Beyond the outcome, the process itself — learning to present complex technical work concisely, thinking strategically about fit, and navigating real hiring pipelines — is professional development that no classroom assignment replicates. Every application I submitted brought me measurably closer to the engineer I am working to become.
